Pet-Friendly Wellington: 25+ Activities, Cafes, Walks & Beaches

Published 10 July 2026 — ZiggySitters NZ — 9 min read

Wellington is one of New Zealand’s most walkable cities, and that makes it brilliant for pet owners. From the wind-swept coast of the south to the bush-clad hills of the western suburbs, there is no shortage of places to explore with your dog. This guide covers the best off-leash parks, dog beaches, pet-friendly cafes, scenic walks, indoor options for rainy days, and seasonal events — all written by locals who actually live here with their dogs.

Off-Leash Dog Parks in Wellington

Wellington City Council manages designated dog exercise areas where your dog can run free. Here are the best ones:

Newtown & Central

  • Newtown Park — Large flat field with room to throw a ball. Popular with local dog owners weekday mornings. Off-leash at designated times.
  • Wellington Botanic Garden ki Paekākā — Leashed dogs welcome on paths. Not off-leash, but a beautiful walk through curated gardens with harbour views.
  • Central Park — Between Brooklyn and the city. Off-leash area in the lower section. Great for midday walks when it is less crowded.

Southern Suburbs

  • Houghton Bay Beach — Small, sheltered beach where dogs can run off-leash. Popular with the Island Bay and Houghton Bay crowd. Check seasonal restrictions.
  • Island Bay Beach — Off-leash along the eastern end. A local favourite for early morning and evening dog walks with views of Taputeranga Island.
  • Owhiro Bay — Rocky and dramatic. Off-leash in designated areas. Great for dogs who like exploring rock pools.

Northern Suburbs

  • Tawa Reserve — Extensive off-leash area along the stream. Well-used by Tawa and Johnsonville dog owners.
  • Johnsonville Park — Good-sized field for off-leash exercise. Popular after work hours.
  • Churton Park — Open grass area with off-leash designation. Usually less crowded than central parks.

Western Suburbs

  • Karori Park — Large playing fields with off-leash areas. The go-to spot for Karori dog owners.
  • Makara Beach — A 20-minute drive from the city centre. Off-leash on the beach. Wild, beautiful, and well worth the trip on a calm day.
Check the rules: Wellington City Council updates off-leash designations and seasonal restrictions regularly. Search “Wellington City Council dog exercise areas” for the current map, or check the council website before visiting a new spot.

Best Dog Walks and Bush Tracks

City and Harbour Walks

  • Wellington Waterfront — Leashed dogs welcome on the promenade from Queens Wharf to Oriental Bay. Paved, flat, and a classic Wellington stroll. Water bowls at several points.
  • Oriental Bay to Balaena Bay — A gentle 30-minute coastal walk. Leashed dogs welcome. Stop at Balaena Bay for a swim.
  • Mount Victoria Lookout Walk — Leashed dogs on the walking tracks. The 360-degree views are worth the climb. Start from Majoribanks Street.

Bush and Reserve Walks

  • Otari-Wilton’s Bush — Leashed dogs on the walking tracks through native forest. The only botanic bush reserve in Wellington. Informative signage about native plants.
  • Town Belt Tracks — Multiple tracks through the green belt between Brooklyn, Aro Valley, and Mt Victoria. Generally leashed, but wide and varied.
  • Red Rocks / Pariwhero — Dramatic coastal walk south of Owhiro Bay. Leashed dogs. Seal colony visible in winter — keep dogs well away from seals.
  • Catchpool Valley, Rimutaka Forest Park — 30 minutes from the city. Leashed dogs on designated tracks. River swimming for dogs in summer. A proper nature escape.

Dog-Friendly Cafes, Bars & Restaurants

Wellington’s cafe culture and dog culture overlap beautifully. These spots welcome dogs in outdoor seating areas, and some even have indoor dog-friendly sections:

Coffee & Breakfast

  • Maranui Cafe (Lyall Bay) — Beachside classic. Dogs on the deck. Super popular after beach walks.
  • The Lighthouse Cafe (Island Bay) — Right near the beach. Dogs welcome outside. Great coffee and brunch.
  • Floriditas (Cuba Street) — A Wellington institution. Dogs outside. Excellent brunch menu.
  • Memphis Belle (Dixon Street) — Small, central, dog-friendly courtyard out back.
  • Loretta (Cuba Street) — All-day dining. Dog-friendly outdoor tables.

Beers & Pubs

  • Garage Project Cellar Door (Dixon Street) — Dog-friendly taproom. Yes, your dog can hang out while you drink craft beer.
  • Hashigo Zake (Dixon Street) — Craft beer bar with dog-friendly outdoor seating.
  • Meat Liquor (Dixon Street) — Burgers, cocktails, and dogs on the patio.
  • The Malthouse (Willis Street) — Dog-friendly outdoor tables. A Wellington beer institution.
Calling ahead helps. Cafe policies change. Ring or check social media before your first visit. Most Wellington cafes are happy to accommodate well-behaved, leashed dogs outdoors, but indoor access varies by venue and season.

Cat-Friendly Wellington (For Cat Owners)

If you have a cat rather than a dog, Wellington still has plenty to offer — even if your cat stays home during outings:

  • Wellington SPCA Op Shop (Newtown) — Cat-themed merchandise and all proceeds support animal welfare.
  • Purrluxe Cat Cafe — Visit resident cats for a caffeine fix. (Check current opening status.)
  • Pet shops with cat play areas — Several pet stores around Wellington have cat toy testing areas where you can try before you buy.
  • Catios and indoor enrichment — If you are building a catio (cat patio), Wellington’s wind means you need sturdy materials. Local builders like Wellington Cat Enclosures can advise on wind-rated netting.

Seasonal Events & Activities

Summer (December–February)

  • Wellington Dog Walk Group meetups — Weekly walks at different locations. Find them on Facebook.
  • Oriental Bay splash — Dogs love the shallows. Early morning before the crowds.
  • Island Bay Beach walks — Longest evenings of the year for sunset walks.

Autumn (March–May)

  • Otari-Wilton’s Bush — Best time for native bush walks. Autumn colours are stunning.
  • Catchpool Valley — Cooler temperatures make for longer, more comfortable hikes with your dog.

Winter (June–August)

  • Indoor dog training classes — Several Wellington clubs offer indoor winter sessions.
  • Doggy day care — When the weather is too wild for walks, day care provides socialisation and exercise.
  • Wellington Waterfront — Wind-sheltered on a northerly day. The promenade is walkable in most weather.

Spring (September–November)

  • Botanic Garden spring walks — Leashed dogs on paths. Cherry blossoms in September and October.
  • Beach season ramps up — Seasonal off-leash restrictions lift at many beaches.
  • Pet vaccination time — Book annual check-ups before the summer holiday rush.

Rainy Day Options

Wellington gets more wet days than most NZ cities. When the weather does not cooperate:

  • Indoor dog training — Wellington Dog Training Club and K9360 offer indoor classes
  • Pet shops — Animates and Petstock on Adelaide Road allow leashed dogs inside
  • DIY enrichment at home — Frozen kongs, snuffle mats, hide-and-seek, and trick training
  • Doggy day care — Multiple centres around Wellington offer half-day and full-day options with indoor play areas

Wellington Pet Rules to Know

  • Dog registration: All dogs must be registered with the local council by 1 July each year. Microchipping is mandatory for dogs registered after 1 July 2006.
  • Leash rules: Dogs must be leashed in all public places unless in a designated off-leash area. Check council maps for boundaries.
  • Picking up: Carry poop bags everywhere. Wellington Council issues fines for not picking up.
  • Cat containment: No region-wide cat containment laws yet, but some subdivisions have covenants. Keep cats indoors at night to protect native wildlife.
  • Beach restrictions: Many beaches have seasonal or time-of-day restrictions on dogs. Always check signage at the entrance.
