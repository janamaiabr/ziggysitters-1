What Is Animal Minding, Exactly?

Animal minding is just a Kiwi way of saying pet sitting. Someone comes to your home — or your pet goes to theirs — and takes care of feeding, walking, medication, and general wellbeing while you're away.

The options break down like this:

Why Remuera Specifically?

Remuera sits between the Domain and Mission Bay, which means it's got some of Auckland's best dog walking territory right on its doorstep. Ōrākei Basin, Hobson Bay walkway, Shore Road reserve — these are spots your dog already knows and loves.

A local animal minder who knows these routes is worth their weight in gold. They won't take your dog somewhere unfamiliar. They know which paths are off-leash friendly, where the water bowls are at the park, and which corner to avoid because there's always a loose staffy there at 4pm.

Remuera also has easy access to emergency vet care. The Newmarket Veterinary Hospital is about seven minutes away, and there's after-hours emergency vet services in Greenlane. A good sitter knows this already — you shouldn't have to tell them.

What to Look for in an Animal Minder

Not all pet minders are created equal. Here's what actually matters:

  1. ID verification. This person is going to be in your home with your animals. At minimum, they should be NZ ID verified. ZiggySitters requires this for every sitter on the platform.
  2. Local knowledge. Do they know your suburb? Can they navigate to the vet without Google Maps? Do they know which parks allow dogs?
  3. Experience with your type of pet. Cat care is very different from dog care. Exotic pets are a whole other story. Ask specifically.
  4. Communication. You want photos. You want updates. You don't want to be the one texting "is my dog okay?" every four hours. A good sitter sends proactive updates.
  5. Reviews from real people. Not testimonials on their own website. Actual reviews from pet owners in Auckland who've used them.

The Difference Between Animal Minding and Kennels

Kennels have their place, but they're a very different experience for your pet. A barking hall of 30 dogs is stressful — even for social dogs. Cats in catteries often stop eating for the first day or two out of sheer anxiety.

In-home animal minding keeps everything familiar. Same bed, same food bowl, same spot on the couch. The only thing that changes is who's filling the bowl. For most pets, that's a much easier transition.

It's also more flexible. Kennel hours are fixed. A house sitter is there whenever your pet needs them. Middle of the night thunderstorm? They're right there. Early morning walk? Already on it.
