Pet-Friendly Accommodation in New Zealand: The Complete Travel Guide

Published on 19 February 2026 | By ZiggySitters

Travelling with your pet across New Zealand is one of the great joys of living in this beautiful country. Whether you are road-tripping through the South Island or exploring the Coromandel Peninsula with your dog, finding the right pet-friendly accommodation can make or break your holiday. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about staying with your furry companion across Aotearoa.

Types of Pet-Friendly Accommodation in New Zealand

Holiday Parks and Campgrounds

Holiday parks are often the most welcoming option for pet owners in New Zealand. Many of the major chains, including TOP 10 Holiday Parks, have pet-friendly policies at select locations. These parks typically offer cabin accommodation, powered sites, and tent sites where dogs are welcome.

The advantages of holiday parks include outdoor space for your pet to explore, on-site facilities like dog wash stations at some locations, and a relaxed atmosphere where animals are a common sight. Expect to pay a small surcharge per pet per night, usually between $5 and $15 NZD.

When booking, always confirm the specific pet policy for your chosen park. Some allow dogs in cabins but not in motel-style units, while others restrict pets to camping areas only. Peak season bookings during summer holidays fill up quickly, so plan well ahead if you are travelling between December and February.

Motels and Motor Lodges

A growing number of motels across New Zealand now accept pets, recognising that many Kiwi families consider their dogs part of the household. Pet-friendly motels are particularly common along popular driving routes such as State Highway 1 and in tourist towns like Hanmer Springs, Kaikoura, and Taupo.

Most pet-friendly motels require that dogs are not left unattended in the room and that owners bring their own bedding for the animal. Some charge a cleaning fee of $20 to $50 NZD. It is always worth calling ahead rather than relying solely on online listings, as policies can change seasonally.

Airbnb and Private Rentals

Airbnb has become a popular option for pet owners, as many hosts specifically welcome animals. When searching on Airbnb, use the pet-friendly filter to narrow your results. Read reviews from other pet owners to gauge how genuinely welcoming the property is.

Private holiday homes and baches are often ideal for pets, offering fenced yards and more space than a standard motel room. In popular areas like Wanaka, Queenstown, and the Bay of Islands, you will find a solid selection of pet-friendly listings year-round.

Department of Conservation (DOC) Campsites

If you enjoy getting off the beaten track, many DOC campsites allow dogs, though there are important restrictions. Dogs are prohibited in most national parks and many conservation areas to protect native wildlife, particularly kiwi and other ground-nesting birds.

Always check the DOC website for specific campsite rules before travelling. Some scenic reserves and forest parks do allow dogs on leads, and these can provide stunning, affordable camping experiences with your pet.

Region-by-Region Guide to Pet-Friendly Stays

Northland and Auckland

Northland is a fantastic destination for pet owners, with numerous beaches that allow dogs during off-peak hours. Accommodation options include pet-friendly holiday parks around Paihia and Mangawhai, along with an increasing number of Airbnb properties in the Kaipara area.

In Auckland, pet-friendly accommodation tends to be more limited in the central city, but the surrounding suburbs and nearby towns like Muriwai and Clevedon offer better options for travelling with animals.

Waikato and Bay of Plenty

The Waikato region offers excellent pet-friendly stays, particularly around Raglan and the Coromandel Peninsula. Tauranga and Mount Maunganui have several motels that accept dogs, making this a great base for exploring the Bay of Plenty coastline.

Central North Island

Taupo and Rotorua are well-served with pet-friendly accommodation. Lake Taupo holiday parks are particularly popular with dog owners who enjoy lakeside walks. The Tongariro region has some options, though access to the national park itself is restricted for dogs.

Wellington Region

The capital has a growing number of pet-friendly Airbnb listings, and the nearby Kapiti Coast and Wairarapa offer motels and holiday parks that welcome animals. The Rimutaka Cycle Trail area is a popular weekend destination for Wellington dog owners.

South Island Highlights

The South Island is a road-tripper\\'s paradise, and finding pet-friendly stops along the way is easier than you might expect. Kaikoura, Hanmer Springs, and the West Coast towns of Hokitika and Greymouth all have motel options for pet owners.

Wanaka stands out as one of the most dog-friendly towns in New Zealand, with numerous accommodation providers welcoming pets and a culture that embraces outdoor activities with animals. Queenstown is trickier but not impossible, with several Airbnb hosts catering to pet owners.

Canterbury and Otago have strong networks of holiday parks and farm stays that welcome dogs, offering a quintessential New Zealand countryside experience.

Essential Tips for Travelling with Pets in NZ

Book Early and Confirm Policies

Pet-friendly rooms are often limited in number, even at accommodation that advertises as welcoming animals. Book as early as possible, especially during school holidays and the summer peak season. Always ring the property directly to confirm their current pet policy, any restrictions on size or breed, and any additional fees.

Pack a Pet Travel Kit

Prepare a travel kit for your pet that includes their regular food, water and food bowls, a familiar blanket or bed, waste bags, any medications, a lead and collar with current identification tags, and a copy of their microchip details. Having familiar items from home helps reduce anxiety during travel.

Respect the Rules

Always keep your dog on a lead in accommodation grounds unless the property specifically provides an off-lead area. Clean up after your pet promptly and report any damage immediately. Being a responsible pet guest helps ensure that more accommodation providers will welcome animals in the future.

Plan Your Route Around Pet Needs

On long drives, stop every two to three hours to let your dog stretch, drink water, and relieve themselves. Research pet-friendly cafes and rest stops along your route. Many New Zealand towns have excellent dog parks that make perfect road trip pit stops.

Consider Pet Insurance for Travel

If you are travelling to remote areas, having pet insurance with emergency vet coverage provides peace of mind. Not every small town has a veterinary clinic, so know the location of the nearest vet along your route before you set off.
