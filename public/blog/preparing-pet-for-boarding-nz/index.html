How to Prepare Your Pet for Boarding or Pet Sitting in NZ

2026-02-04 · 7 min read · ZiggySitters Team

Leaving your pet with a sitter for the first time can feel nerve-wracking. But with the right preparation, both you and your pet can have a stress-free experience. Here is your complete checklist for NZ pet owners.

1. Update Vaccinations and Microchip

Make sure your pet’s vaccinations are current. In NZ, dogs should be up to date with their annual booster (C5 or C7 vaccination). Ensure your microchip details are current on the NZCAR (New Zealand Companion Animal Register) — this is required by law for dogs in NZ.

2. Do a Meet and Greet

Always arrange a meet and greet with your sitter before the booking. Let your pet sniff around, get comfortable, and see how they interact. ZiggySitters encourages free meet and greets — it is the best way to build trust.

3. Prepare a Care Sheet

Write down your pet’s daily routine: feeding times, portion sizes, favourite treats, exercise needs, medications, and any quirks. The more detail you provide, the more comfortable your pet will be.

4. Pack Familiar Items

Bring your pet’s bed, favourite toy, and a piece of clothing that smells like you. Familiar scents reduce anxiety significantly, especially for dogs.

5. Leave Emergency Contacts

Provide your vet’s details, an emergency contact number, and your travel itinerary. Make sure your sitter knows where the nearest after-hours vet is located.

6. Keep Goodbyes Short

Long, emotional goodbyes can make your pet anxious. Keep it brief and upbeat. Your pet will pick up on your confidence.
