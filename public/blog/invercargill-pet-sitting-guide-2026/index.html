Invercargill Pet Sitting Guide 2026: Everything You Need to Know

By ZiggySitters • Updated March 2026 • 10 min read

Quick Summary: Invercargill is a genuinely pet-friendly city with exceptional outdoor spaces, a tight-knit community, and growing demand for quality in-home pet care. This guide covers local dog walking spots, pet sitting costs, winter care tips, and how to find a trusted sitter in Southland.

Invercargill does not often top the list when New Zealanders talk about great cities for pets, but it should. NZ's southernmost city offers something that Auckland and Wellington cannot: wide-open spaces, uncrowded beaches, and a community feel where people genuinely look out for one another — including each other's animals.

For pet owners in Invercargill and the wider Southland region, the challenge is not finding things to do with your dog. It is finding reliable, experienced pet care when you need to travel, work long hours, or simply need a hand. This guide is designed to help you do exactly that.

Pets in Invercargill: The Local Landscape

Invercargill has a strong pet-owning culture. The city's residential streets are full of dogs, and the combination of large sections, nearby rural land, and world-class beaches makes it an excellent environment for active breeds. Cats are also hugely popular — the city's mild but cool climate suits independent indoor-outdoor cats well.

The pet care industry in Invercargill has grown considerably over the past few years. Local vets, groomers, and pet sitters serve a community that genuinely invests in animal welfare. The Southland SPCA is active and respected, and the city has a culture of responsible pet ownership.

Popular Breeds in Invercargill and Southland

Given the climate and outdoor lifestyle, working and sporting breeds are especially popular in Southland. You will see a lot of:

  • Labrador Retrievers and Golden Retrievers
  • Border Collies and other herding breeds
  • German Shepherds
  • Staffordshire Bull Terriers
  • Huntaways (the quintessential NZ working dog)
  • Smaller companion breeds in the city centre: Shih Tzus, Bichon Frises, Cavoodles

Best Dog Walking Spots in Invercargill

One of the great things about Invercargill is the variety of walking environments within easy reach. A good pet sitter should know these areas well — they are part of what makes this city special for dog owners.

Oreti Beach

One of the longest beaches in New Zealand and an off-leash paradise for dogs. Wide, flat, and perfect for a long run. Watch the tide and check conditions before letting small dogs run freely.

Queens Park

The heart of Invercargill, Queens Park has designated off-leash areas, well-maintained paths, and a lovely duck pond. A reliable daily walk option close to the city centre.

Sandy Point Reserve

A 900-hectare reserve with walking and mountain bike trails, forest walks, and open grassland. Excellent for energetic breeds that need real exercise. Accessed via Dunns Road.

Waihopai River Walkway

A pleasant riverside walk through the city, connecting several suburbs. Suitable for all breeds and weather conditions. Popular with morning walkers.

Thomsons Bush

A small but beautiful remnant of native podocarp forest in the heart of Invercargill. Great for sensory enrichment walks with curious dogs.

Bluff Hill

A short drive south, Bluff Hill offers coastal views and interesting terrain. Ideal for confident, energetic dogs. The port area is also worth exploring on the return.

Tip for Sitters: When caring for Invercargill dogs, always check the weather before Oreti Beach visits. The beach is exposed and conditions can change quickly — what starts as a clear morning can become cold and blustery by midday. Carry a dog towel for wet sand days.

Winter Care in Invercargill

Invercargill winters are the most significant variable in pet care for this city. Temperatures regularly drop to 2-4°C, and frosts are common from May through September. What does this mean in practice?

  • Short-haired breeds: Dogs like Staffordshire Bull Terriers, Greyhounds, and Whippets need a well-fitted coat for outdoor winter walks. They feel the cold significantly.
  • Senior dogs: Cold weather exacerbates arthritis. Older dogs may be slower to warm up in the mornings and need a heated sleeping spot away from draughts.
  • Puppies: Young dogs under 6 months have less body mass and temperature regulation. Keep outdoor sessions brief in cold weather and make sure they are warm and dry before settling for the night.
  • Cats: Even cats that are outdoor-confident in summer may appreciate an indoor option during winter nights. Ensure cat flaps are well-insulated or provide a warm enclosed space in the garage or shed.
Frost Warning: Metal surfaces (water bowls, kennels, outdoor furniture) can become dangerously cold overnight. Check that outdoor water has not frozen, and ensure kennels are insulated from the ground up. A raised bed with a thick fleece liner makes a significant difference.

How to Find a Trusted Pet Sitter in Invercargill

The best pet sitting relationships are built on trust and local knowledge. Here is a practical approach to finding and vetting a pet sitter in Invercargill.

Step 1: Use a Reputable Platform

ZiggySitters lists verified pet sitters across New Zealand, including Invercargill and the Southland region. Sitters on the platform have completed profile verification, and you can read reviews from other local owners before booking.

Step 2: Ask the Right Questions

Before confirming a booking, speak with the sitter — either by phone or in-person. Good questions to ask:

  • How much experience do you have with [your breed/species]?
  • What is your routine for cold or wet weather walks?
  • How do you handle a pet emergency?
  • Are you insured?
  • How often will you send updates and photos?
  • What is your backup plan if you are unwell?

Step 3: Do a Trial Visit

Always arrange a meet-and-greet before your first trip away. This lets your pet adjust to the sitter while you are still present, and gives you the chance to walk through your pet's routine in your own home. It also lets you observe how the sitter interacts with your animal.

Step 4: Prepare a Detailed Care Sheet

A good care sheet removes ambiguity and sets your sitter up for success. Include:

  • Feeding amounts, times, and food storage location
  • Walk routes and any areas to avoid
  • Behaviour quirks (reactive to cyclists, scared of thunder, etc.)
  • Medications and administration instructions
  • Vet details: your regular vet and the nearest emergency clinic
  • How to reach you (and a backup contact)
  • Any security codes or key instructions

Vets and Emergency Pet Care in Invercargill

Knowing your local vet options is essential, especially when leaving a pet in a sitter's care. Invercargill has a solid network of veterinary clinics serving the city and surrounding rural areas.

When Using a Pet Sitter, Provide:

  • Your regular vet's name and phone number
  • Whether the sitter has written authorisation to seek treatment
  • Your pet insurance policy number (if applicable)
  • A spending limit for emergency treatment decisions
Good Practice: Leave a signed note with your pet sitter authorising veterinary treatment in an emergency. Most NZ vets will accept a written note from the owner, and having this prepared avoids delays in urgent situations.

Special Considerations for Invercargill Pet Owners

Farm Dogs and Working Breeds

Many Southland pet owners have dogs with working breed heritage — Border Collies, Huntaways, Kelpies. These dogs have higher exercise and mental stimulation needs than the average companion dog. When briefing a pet sitter, be honest about how much exercise your dog actually needs. A working breed that is under-stimulated will find its own entertainment, and that is rarely good news.

Cats in Southland

Cat owners in Invercargill should be aware that Southland District Council rules on cat management may differ from Invercargill City Council regulations. Check local bylaws if you are on the urban fringe. Most cat sitters in Invercargill offer twice-daily visits covering feeding, litter, and a brief play session — this is usually sufficient for confident adult cats.

Holiday Season Demand

Like most NZ cities, demand for pet sitters in Invercargill peaks sharply over Christmas and New Year. Many Invercargill families travel north to visit family or holiday in warmer regions, leaving a surge in demand for local pet care. Book early — ideally 3-4 weeks ahead for peak periods.

Winters are cold and frosty. Short-haired breeds, puppies, and seniors need warm coats and insulated sleeping areas. Check that outdoor water has not frozen, and limit time outdoors during frost events.
