Pet-Friendly Wineries & Wine Trails in New Zealand

Published 6 May 2026 — ZiggySitters NZ

New Zealand is world-renowned for its Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Noir, and stunning vineyard landscapes. The good news? Many of our cellar doors roll out the welcome mat for well-behaved dogs. This guide covers the best pet-friendly wine regions, vineyard etiquette, and how to plan the ultimate NZ wine trail with your four-legged companion.

Why NZ Wine Country Is Great for Dogs

New Zealand vineyards are typically set on expansive rural properties with open spaces, walking tracks, and outdoor tasting areas. Unlike urban restaurants, many wineries embrace the rural lifestyle and welcome dogs on their grounds. The climate from spring through autumn makes outdoor tasting a comfortable experience for both humans and pets, and the laid-back Kiwi culture means leash-friendly spaces are the norm rather than the exception.

Marlborough — Sauvignon Blanc Capital

Marlborough produces over two-thirds of New Zealand wine and the flat terrain makes it one of the most dog-friendly wine regions in the country. Many cellar doors have grassy lawns where your dog can relax while you taste.

Top Pet-Friendly Cellar Doors

  • Cloudy Bay Vineyards — Dogs welcome on the lawn and outdoor areas. Water bowls provided on request. A Marlborough classic with a stunning setting.
  • Brancott Estate Heritage Centre — Expansive grounds with panoramic views. Well-behaved dogs on lead are welcome outside. The walking track around the property is perfect for a pre-tasting stroll.
  • Villa Maria Marlborough — Outdoor tasting area is dog-friendly. Staff are accustomed to canine visitors and happy to provide water.
  • Serres Estate — A boutique vineyard where the owners themselves have dogs. Very welcoming atmosphere with shaded outdoor seating.
  • Wither Hills — Large lawn area with vineyard views. Dogs on leads are welcome at outdoor tables.
Tip: The Marlborough Wine & Food Festival (February) allows dogs in the outer grounds but not inside the main marquee. Arrive early for a relaxed experience and bring shade for your dog.

Hawke\'s Bay — Art Deco Meets Wine Country

Hawke\'s Bay is New Zealand\'s second-largest wine region and its warm climate makes it a year-round destination. The Gimblett Gravels and Bridge Pa Triangle produce world-class Bordeaux blends and Syrah, while the surrounding landscape offers excellent dog walking.

Top Pet-Friendly Cellar Doors

  • Church Road Winery — Historic winery with beautiful grounds. Dogs on leads are welcome in the outdoor courtyard and museum area.
  • Clearview Estate Winery — A coastal vineyard with a genuinely pet-friendly attitude. Outdoor seating among the vines with water bowls available.
  • Elephant Hill Winery — Stunning coastal location near Cape Kidnappers. Dogs welcome on the outdoor terrace overlooking the ocean. One of the most scenic tasting experiences in NZ.
  • Ngatarawa Wines — Surrounded by tree-lined avenues and lakes. Dogs can stretch their legs on the grounds while you enjoy the cellar door.
  • Sileni Estates — Large outdoor areas with vineyard views. Well-behaved dogs on leads are welcome. The restaurant terrace is a great spot for lunch with your pet.
Tip: Combine your Hawke\'s Bay wine trip with a walk along the Napier Marine Parade or Ahuriri Estuary Walk — both are dog-friendly and within minutes of the wine trail.

Central Otago — Pinot Noir Paradise

Central Otago is the world\'s southernmost wine region and home to New Zealand\'s finest Pinot Noir. The dramatic landscapes of Gibbston Valley, Bannockburn, and Wanaka make this an extraordinary place to explore with a dog.

Top Pet-Friendly Cellar Doors

  • Gibbston Valley Winery — The most famous cellar door in the valley. Dogs are welcome on the grounds and outdoor seating. The adjacent Gibbston River Trail is a scenic dog-friendly walk along the Kawarau River.
  • Peregrine Wines — Architecturally stunning winery set among the hills. Dogs on leads are welcome on the outdoor terrace.
  • Mount Difficulty Wines — Perched above the Cromwell Basin with panoramic views. Outdoor seating welcomes dogs, and the surrounding landscape is perfect for a short walk.
  • Rippon Vineyard — Lake Wanaka backdrop makes this one of the most photographed vineyards in NZ. Dogs on leads welcome outside. The lakefront walk nearby is fantastic for dogs.
  • Chard Farm Winery — Accessed via a dramatic road cut into the cliff face. Dogs welcome in the outdoor area. The setting alone is worth the visit.

Waiheke Island — Auckland\'s Island Wine Escape

Just a 40-minute ferry ride from Auckland, Waiheke Island is a boutique wine destination with over 30 wineries and olive groves. The island lifestyle is inherently pet-friendly, and many vineyards welcome dogs in their outdoor areas.

Top Pet-Friendly Cellar Doors

  • Mudbrick Vineyard — One of Waiheke\'s most iconic wineries. Dogs on leads welcome on the outdoor terrace with Hauraki Gulf views.
  • Stonyridge Vineyard — Mediterranean-style setting. Dogs welcome outside. The relaxed island vibe makes this a stress-free stop.
  • Cable Bay Vineyards — Stunning ocean views with outdoor seating. Well-behaved dogs welcome. One of Waiheke\'s most stylish cellar doors.
  • Batch Winery — The highest vineyard on the island with panoramic views. Very dog-friendly with outdoor tasting among the vines.
  • Peacock Sky Wine & Art — A creative space combining wine and art. Dogs welcome in the sculpture garden and outdoor area.
Important: Dogs travelling on the Fullers360 ferry to Waiheke must be on a lead and remain in designated outdoor areas. Book pet tickets in advance during peak summer season.

Martinborough — Wairarapa\'s Wine Village

Martinborough is a compact, walkable wine village less than 90 minutes from Wellington. Almost all cellar doors are within walking distance of the village square, and many welcome dogs in their gardens and outdoor spaces.

Top Pet-Friendly Cellar Doors

  • Ata Rangi — One of NZ\'s most respected Pinot Noir producers. Dogs welcome in the garden courtyard.
  • Palliser Estate — Beautiful grounds with a relaxed atmosphere. Dogs on leads welcome outside.
  • Martinborough Vineyard — The original vineyard in the region. Dogs welcome in the outdoor area.
  • Tirohana Estate — Boutique vineyard with a personal touch. Dog-friendly garden setting for tastings.
  • Cabbage Tree Vineyard — Small and welcoming. The owners love dogs and are happy to accommodate well-behaved pets.
Tip: Martinborough is one of the few NZ wine regions where you can walk between cellar doors. Leave the car behind and enjoy a safe, leisurely tasting tour with your dog on lead through the village.

Vineyard Etiquette for Dog Owners

Following a few simple rules ensures vineyards continue to welcome dogs:

  1. Always keep your dog on a lead — even well-trained dogs can be startled by wildlife, other dogs, or machinery on the property.
  2. Bring your own water bowl — while many wineries provide water, having your own ensures your dog stays hydrated on warm days.
  3. Respect the vines — never let your dog enter vine rows. Dogs can carry diseases and damage fragile grape vines.
  4. Clean up after your dog — always carry waste bags. Agricultural properties take hygiene seriously.
  5. Ask before entering — even if a winery is listed as pet-friendly, policies can change. A quick phone call saves disappointment.
  6. Avoid harvest season disruptions — during March-May harvest, vineyards are busy with machinery and workers. Dogs may be less welcome during this period.
  7. Keep dogs away from grape skins and pips — grapes and raisins are toxic to dogs. Never let your pet eat anything found on the vineyard floor.
Toxic Warning: Grapes and raisins are highly toxic to dogs and can cause kidney failure. At vineyards, keep your dog away from grape debris, especially during and after harvest when fallen fruit is common on the ground.

Best Seasons for Wine Trail Adventures

Spring (September – November)

The vineyards come alive with fresh green growth. Temperatures are mild (12-18°C in most regions), making it comfortable for dogs. Cellar doors are quieter, giving you a more personal experience. Spring lambing on neighbouring farms means keeping your dog on lead is especially important.

Summer (December – February)

Peak season for both tourism and wine tasting. Outdoor seating areas are in full use and most welcoming to dogs. Be mindful of heat — bring plenty of water and plan tastings for cooler morning hours. Pavement and stone surfaces can burn paws in Hawke\'s Bay and Central Otago where temperatures regularly exceed 30°C.

Autumn (March – May)

Harvest season brings stunning colour to the vines but also means increased activity. Many vineyards restrict dog access during the busiest harvest weeks (typically mid-March to mid-April). After harvest, the vineyards are peaceful again with mild autumn temperatures — ideal for relaxed visits.

Winter (June – August)

Most cellar doors reduce hours or close. Those that remain open typically host tastings indoors where dogs are not permitted. If visiting in winter, confirm pet policies before travelling. Martinborough and Waiheke have the best winter options.

Planning a Multi-Day Wine Trail with Your Dog

New Zealand wine regions are spread across both islands, making road trips the best way to experience them. When planning a multi-day trip:

  • Book pet-friendly accommodation early — wine region motels and holiday homes fill up fast, especially from December to March.
  • Plan 2-3 cellar doors per day maximum — your dog needs breaks, exercise, and downtime between stops.
  • Include non-wine activities — dog-friendly beaches, river walks, and off-lead parks near each wine region give your pet variety.
  • Never leave your dog in the car — NZ summer temperatures inside a parked car can reach fatal levels within minutes.
  • Consider a designated driver or tour — some wine tour operators in Marlborough and Hawke\'s Bay allow dogs on their vehicles. This means you can enjoy tastings without worrying about driving.
