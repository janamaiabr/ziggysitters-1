Pet-Friendly Day Trips in the Bay of Plenty: 12 Adventures with Your Dog

Published 22 June 2026 • 10 min read • ZiggySitters NZ

The Bay of Plenty lives up to its name — sunshine, surf, and some of the most dog-friendly spots in New Zealand. Whether you are a local looking for a weekend adventure or visiting from out of town, here are 12 day trips that welcome your four-legged mate along for the ride.

Before You Go: Bay of Plenty Dog Rules

New Zealand councils update their dog bylaws regularly, and the Bay of Plenty is no exception. Before heading out, keep these basics in mind:

  • Leash rules vary by beach and season. Many beaches allow off-leash before 10 AM and after 7 PM during summer (December–March), with more freedom in winter.
  • Check council websites for Tauranga, Western Bay of Plenty, and Whakatane district rules before each trip.
  • Always carry poo bags — on-the-spot fines of $300+ apply in most Bay of Plenty districts.
  • Farm dogs and working dogs have different rules; this guide focuses on companion pets.
Pro tip: Download the local council is dog leash map before you go. Tauranga City Council and Western Bay of Plenty District Council both publish updated maps online showing designated off-leash areas and seasonal restrictions.

1. Mount Maunganui Main Beach & Base Track

BeachWalkCafe

The iconic Mount is the Bay is centrepiece, and dogs are welcome on the beach with leash restrictions. Walk the flat base track around the Mount (3.4 km loop) — it is paved, scenic, and perfect for morning exercise. Early morning (before 10 AM in summer) is your best bet for off-leash time on the sand.

Dog-friendly cafe: The Pilot Bay end has several waterfront cafes with outdoor seating that welcome dogs. Try Mount Maunganui Beach Cafe for a post-walk coffee.

Parking: Paid parking along Marine Parade. Arrive before 9 AM on weekends.

2. Papamoa Beach & Dune Reserve

BeachOff-Leash

Less crowded than the Mount, Papamoa Beach stretches for kilometres and has generous off-leash windows. The dune reserve behind the beach provides a softer surface for older dogs or those with joint issues. Access from Pacific View Road or Parton Road.

Best time: Early morning year-round. Winter offers more off-leash freedom.

Amenities: Toilets and water fountains at main access points. Bring your own dog water bowl.

3. McLaren Falls Park

Bush WalkWaterfallPicnic

Just 10 minutes from Tauranga, McLaren Falls Park is a 190-hectare slice of paradise. Dogs are welcome on-leash throughout the park. Walk the waterfall track (easy, 20 minutes), explore the lakeside paths, or set up a picnic under the trees. The waterfalls are stunning after rain.

Tip: The Christmas lights display runs from late December — dogs are welcome but it gets crowded. Go early in the evening.

Entry: Free. Dogs on-leash only. No swimming for dogs in the lake.

4. Katikati Haiku Pathway & Murals

Town WalkArtEasy

Katikati is New Zealand is only Irish town and home to a stunning haiku pathway along the Uretara Stream. The path is paved, flat, and perfect for a leisurely dog walk. The town is famous for its outdoor murals — wander the main street and discover over 40 works of art. Most cafes in town have dog-friendly outdoor seating.

Distance: Haiku pathway is 1.5 km one way. Allow 1–2 hours for the full town wander.

Cafe pick: The Falls Cafe on the main highway has a dog-friendly courtyard.

5. Waihi Beach to Orakawa Track

Coastal WalkModerate

This 3 km coastal track winds from Waihi Beach through native bush to the secluded Orakawa Beach. It is one of the most beautiful walks in the region, and dogs on-leash are welcome. The track has some steep sections and roots — not suitable for very young puppies or dogs with mobility issues. The beach at the end is pristine and usually empty.

Important: Check the tide times before you go. Parts of the track can be slippery after rain. Bring water for your dog — there is none on the track.

6. TECT All Terrain Park

Off-LeashMountain BikeAdventure

Located between Tauranga and Rotorua, TECT Park is a 1200-hectare recreation zone with walking tracks, mountain bike trails, and off-leash dog areas. It is the best spot in the Bay for high-energy dogs that need to really run. The mixed terrain — bush, open fields, and gravel paths — provides excellent enrichment.

Dog rules: Off-leash in designated areas, on-leash on shared tracks with mountain bikers. Check the map at the entrance.

Facilities: Toilets, car park, picnic tables. Bring everything else you need — it is remote.

7. Omokoroa Domain & Esplanade

BeachFlat WalkFamily

This little harbour settlement on the Coromandel side of Tauranga Harbour is a hidden gem. The esplanade walk is flat, scenic, and dog-friendly on-leash. The harbour beach is calm — ideal for dogs that are nervous about surf. Omokoroa is also home to the popular boat club, which has a dog-friendly deck for lunch.

Distance: Esplanade walk is approximately 2 km return. Easy for all fitness levels.

8. Whakatane & Ohope Beach

BeachTown WalkOff-Leash

Whakatane is officially New Zealand is sunshine capital, and Ohope Beach (just over the hill) is a 10 km stretch of golden sand that is hugely popular with dog owners. The Whakatane River mouth to West End section allows off-leash before 10 AM and after 7 PM in summer. The Nga Tapuwae o Toi walkway from the Whakatane heads is a stunning coastal track where dogs on-leash are welcome.

Must-do: Grab fish and chips from Whakatane and eat them on Ohope Beach with your dog at sunset. Classic NZ summer.

9. Te Puke & the No. 1 Roto Iti Walk

Lake WalkEasyNature

Te Puke is known as the kiwifruit capital, but it also has lovely walking tracks around Roto Iti (Little Lake). The track is flat, shaded, and dog-friendly on-leash. It is a peaceful 2 km loop — perfect for older dogs or a quiet morning stroll. Combine it with a visit to the Te Puke Kiwifruit Country tours (dogs not permitted on the tour itself, but the grounds are dog-friendly).

10. Tauranga Waterfront & Strand

Urban WalkCafeShopping

Tauranga is waterfront is one of the most dog-friendly urban areas in the Bay. The Strand promenade runs along the harbour, and many of the cafes and restaurants have dog-friendly outdoor seating. Walk from the Strand to Memorial Park and back for a 3 km flat harbour-side walk. On Saturdays, the Tauranga Farmers Market at Memorial Park is dog-friendly on-leash.

Cafe picks: The Strand cafes with dog-friendly patios include The Coffee Club, Starbuckle, and The Rising Tide.

11. Maketu Beach & Estuary

BeachEstuaryQuiet

Maketu is a small coastal settlement with a long, quiet beach and an expansive estuary. It is far less crowded than the Mount or Papamoa, making it ideal for dogs that get anxious around crowds. The estuary walk is flat and offers great birdwatching. Off-leash is permitted on the beach outside of restricted bird nesting areas (check signage).

Wildlife note: Maketu is an important area for dotterel and other shorebirds. Respect all signage about nesting areas and keep dogs well away from marked zones, especially September–March.

12. Waihi Gorge Walkway

Bush WalkRiverModerate

A stunning hidden gem near Waihi, this 4 km return walk follows the Ohinemuri River through a dramatic gorge with sheer rock walls and swimming holes. Dogs on-leash are welcome. The track is well-formed but has some narrow sections above the river. It is a favourite with locals and barely known to tourists. Best after rain when the river is flowing and the gorge is at its most dramatic.

Getting there: From Waihi, head towards Waikino on State Highway 2. The car park is signposted on the left.

Day Trip Packing List for Your Dog

  • Water and collapsible bowl — many Bay beaches have no fresh water
  • Poo bags — bring more than you think you will need
  • Leash and harness — even at off-leash areas, you need quick control near roads or wildlife
  • Towel — for sandy, wet, or muddy dogs before they get in the car
  • Dog first aid kit — bandages, antiseptic, tick remover
  • Treats — for recall practice and good behaviour rewards
  • Sunscreen — for short-coated or light-skinned dogs (yes, dogs get sunburned too)
  • Council leash map &mdash> saved offline on your phone

Seasonal Tips for Bay of Plenty Day Trips

Summer (December–February)

  • Go early or late to avoid heatstroke — the Bay gets hot (25–30°C regularly)
  • Hot sand burns paws. If you cannot hold your hand on the sand for 5 seconds, it is too hot for your dog is feet
  • Stick to morning walks and beach visits before 10 AM
  • Bring more water than you think you need — dogs dehydrate faster than humans

Winter (June–August)

  • More off-leash freedom on most beaches
  • Fewer crowds mean more relaxed walks
  • Shorter days — plan to be back before dark
  • Watch for slippery tracks after rain, especially Waihi Gorge and Orakawa

Spring (September–November)

  • Lambing season — keep dogs well away from paddocks with lambs
  • Beach nesting birds start arriving — respect all roped-off areas
  • Great weather for longer walks — not too hot, not too cold

What About When You Cannot Take Your Dog?

Not every trip is suitable for pets — White Island tours, for example, do not allow dogs. When you need to head out without your mate, a trusted local pet sitter keeps them happy, exercised, and safe at home.
