Pet Sitting in Rotorua 2026: Your Complete Local Guide

By ZiggySitters NZ  |  March 2, 2026  |  11 min read

Rotorua is a city unlike any other in New Zealand — geothermal wonders, lush forests, and a vibrant community make it a fantastic place to live with pets. But it also comes with unique considerations that every pet owner (and pet sitter) needs to know. This guide covers everything: finding a trustworthy sitter, dog-friendly parks, geothermal safety, and what good pet care looks like in the Rotorua Lakes district.

Why Pet Sitting Matters in Rotorua

Rotorua residents travel — whether for work to Auckland or Hamilton, holidays across the country, or short getaways to nearby Taupo and the Coromandel. When you leave, your pets need consistent, attentive care. Kennels are an option, but many pet owners in Rotorua prefer in-home pet sitting or home-based sitters for the personal attention and the familiar routine it provides.

Cats especially struggle with relocation to kennels. Dogs with anxiety or older pets with health conditions do better in a home environment. For Rotorua pet owners, finding a local sitter who understands the area — including its unique geothermal environment — is a real advantage.

The Rotorua Difference: Geothermal Safety for Pets

This is the topic most city guides skip — but in Rotorua, it is non-negotiable. The city sits on one of the world's most active geothermal fields. Steam vents, thermal pools, and geothermally active ground are present throughout the region, including in some public parks and residential areas.

⚠ Geothermal Hazards for Dogs and Cats:
  • Thermal pools and hot ground: Dogs are naturally curious and may approach steaming vents or hot ground. Contact with geothermal water can cause severe burns. Always keep dogs on lead near any steaming or bubbling areas.
  • Hydrogen sulphide gas: At low concentrations, this gas smells like rotten eggs. At higher concentrations it is dangerous for both humans and animals. Dogs can be more sensitive than humans because they breathe closer to the ground. If you notice a strong sulphur smell in an enclosed space, leave immediately.
  • Geothermal water: Some ground water in Rotorua has elevated levels of minerals and compounds that are harmful if ingested. Do not allow dogs to drink from unknown puddles, pools, or streams near geothermal areas.
  • Unstable ground: In some areas, the ground crust over a thermal area is thin. Stick to marked paths.

A good Rotorua pet sitter should be aware of these local hazards. When interviewing sitters, ask specifically whether they know how to identify and avoid geothermal areas during walks. This is local knowledge that matters.

Dog-Friendly Parks and Walks in Rotorua

Despite its geothermal character, Rotorua has excellent dog-friendly outdoor spaces. Here are some of the best areas for pet exercise during a sitting stay.

Off-Lead Dog Exercise Areas

Rotorua Lakes Council designates specific off-lead exercise zones. Always check the current Rotorua Lakes Council website for updated rules, as designations can change.

  • Kuirau Park (outer areas): Rotorua's free thermal park has designated areas away from the active geothermal features where dogs are permitted on lead. Always follow signage — this is not the place for off-lead running.
  • Whakarewarewa Forest (Redwoods): One of the great outdoor assets of Rotorua, the Redwood Forest offers stunning trails through towering California redwoods. Dogs on lead are welcome on many tracks. The smells and forest environment are a sensory delight for dogs. Check specific trail rules with Rotorua Canopy Tours or the Scion website.
  • Lake Rotorua foreshore: Parts of the lakefront have walking paths where leashed dogs are welcome. The lake views are spectacular. Avoid areas with strong sulphur activity.
  • Hamurana Springs Recreation Reserve: Located north of Rotorua, this stunning reserve features crystal-clear spring water and a giant redwood grove. Dogs on lead are generally welcome in the picnic and walk areas (check current access rules).
  • Ngongotaha and Holdens Bay: Residential suburbs with good walking streets and local reserves suitable for daily exercise during a sitting stay.
💡 Local Tip: The Rotorua Trail Network has over 170km of trails around the city and surrounding forest. Many are suitable for dog walks. Download the Rotorua Trails app or pick up a free map from the i-SITE visitor centre before heading out with your dog.

Water Access for Dogs

Lake Rotorua and the surrounding lakes offer water access, but a few cautions apply:

  • Always check water quality notices before allowing your dog to swim. Lake Rotorua has periodic algal bloom warnings, particularly in summer.
  • Rinse your dog thoroughly with clean water after any lake swim to remove algae, minerals, and any contaminants.
  • Lakes Rotoiti and Rotoehu to the east generally have better water quality for recreational swimming.

Rotorua's Suburbs: A Pet-Sitting Perspective

Rotorua's suburbs have different characters, and knowing which area your sitter covers matters for exercise quality.

Fenton Park & Glenholme

Central suburbs close to Kuirau Park. Good footpaths and quiet streets for lead walks. Easy access to the lakefront.

Ngongotaha

Leafy semi-rural suburb northwest of the city. Excellent for dogs who need space. Close to Hamurana Springs.

Holdens Bay & Rotokawa

Eastern lakeside suburbs. Good access to quieter parts of the lake foreshore for walking and swimming.

Owhata & Springfield

Residential areas with local parks. Convenient for everyday walks without geothermal proximity.

Lynmore & Reporoa Rd areas

South of the centre, close to the Redwood Forest. Ideal for active dogs who love forest walks.

Sunhaven & Fairy Springs

Northern suburbs. Fairy Springs area has good walking tracks near Rainbow Springs.

What to Look for in a Rotorua Pet Sitter

Beyond the general qualities of a good sitter — experience, reliability, genuine love of animals — a Rotorua-based sitter should bring some local-specific knowledge.

Must-Have Qualities

  • Geothermal awareness: They should know which local parks and walks are safe for pets. This is a genuine differentiator in Rotorua.
  • Emergency vet access: Know which clinic your sitter would use in an emergency. Rotorua has several vet practices — confirm your sitter has the contact details and knows the fastest route.
  • Reviews from local pet owners: Someone who has cared for Rotorua pets before understands the local environment. Look for sitter profiles with reviews from the Rotorua Lakes district specifically.
  • Clear communication: A sitter who sends daily updates (a photo or a quick message) gives you peace of mind while you're away. Ask upfront whether this is part of their service.

Questions to Ask Before Booking

  1. Which walking areas do you use regularly with dogs in Rotorua?
  2. How do you handle geothermal areas during walks?
  3. Which vet clinic would you use in an emergency?
  4. How do you update owners during the stay?
  5. Have you cared for pets with medical needs or medications before?
  6. What happens if you have an emergency yourself and cannot continue the sit?

Cat Sitting in Rotorua: Special Considerations

Rotorua's geothermal environment creates some unique considerations for outdoor cats. Gardens in some parts of the city have geothermally warmed ground — which cats find very appealing. While a warm patch of ground might seem harmless, thermally active areas can have dangerous gas emissions at ground level.

If your cat has outdoor access and you live near a thermally active area, discuss this with your sitter. It may be worth restricting outdoor access during a sitting stay as an extra precaution — especially at night when gases can concentrate in low-lying areas.

Cat Sitting Checklist for Rotorua Owners:
  • Advise sitter of any thermally active areas in your garden or immediate neighbourhood
  • Discuss whether outdoor access should be restricted at night
  • Ensure sitter has your cat's microchip number and vet contact
  • Leave a week's supply of food to avoid any supply gaps
  • Ask sitter to check water bowls twice daily — cats in warm indoor environments drink more

Preparing Your Pet for a Rotorua Sitting Stay

Good preparation makes a significant difference to how well your pet settles with a sitter. A few days before you leave:

  • Arrange a meet-and-greet so your pet and sitter can get acquainted before the pressure of a departure day.
  • Write a care sheet covering feeding schedule, medications, exercise routine, known fears (thunder, fireworks), and emergency contacts.
  • Leave familiar bedding and a worn item of clothing with your scent to comfort your pet in your absence.
  • Make sure vaccinations and parasite prevention are current — this protects your pet and the sitter's other animals.
  • If your pet takes medication, pre-measure doses and leave clear written instructions. Never assume verbal instructions will be remembered exactly.

Pet-Friendly Accommodation Near Rotorua

If you're visiting Rotorua with your pet rather than leaving them behind, the region has a growing number of pet-friendly accommodation options. Several holiday parks around Lake Rotorua accept well-behaved dogs. Properties in Ngongotaha and the rural areas south of the city tend to be more pet-accommodating than central hotels. Always confirm pet policies directly when booking — rules vary widely.
