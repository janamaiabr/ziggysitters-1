Pet-Friendly Moving Companies in New Zealand: The Complete Guide

Published 12 June 2026 • 10 min read • ZiggySitters NZ

Moving house is one of life\u2019s most stressful events \u2014 and that stress multiplies when you\u2019re moving with pets. From finding a moving company that genuinely cares about your fur family to ensuring your cat or dog arrives at your new home safe and calm, here\u2019s everything New Zealand pet owners need to know.

Why Most Moving Companies Aren\u2019t Great with Pets

Standard furniture movers are brilliant at shifting couches and boxes. But pets? They\u2019re a different story. Most moving companies in New Zealand don\u2019t specialise in animal transport, and here\u2019s why that matters:

  • No temperature control: Standard trucks can get dangerously hot in summer \u2014 especially on long hauls between Auckland and Christchurch.
  • Loud, stressful environments: The clatter of furniture being loaded is terrifying for animals already anxious about change.
  • No animal handling expertise: Movers are trained in logistics, not in calming a stressed cat or reading a dog\u2019s anxiety signals.
  • Insurance gaps: Most moving insurance covers furniture damage, not veterinary bills if an animal is injured during transit.
Key distinction: A \u201Cpet-friendly mover\u201D is not the same as a pet transport service. Pet-friendly movers will take extra care around your animals and may allow them in the cab. Pet transport services specialise in moving animals. You often need both.

What Makes a Moving Company \u201CPet-Friendly\u201D?

Not every company that says they\u2019re \u201Cpet-friendly\u201D actually is. Look for these genuine indicators:

1. Flexible Scheduling Around Your Pet

A truly pet-friendly mover will work with you to minimise your pet\u2019s stress. That might mean loading your belongings first so you can keep your pet in a quiet room until the last possible moment, or scheduling the move during cooler hours in summer.

2. Willingness to Accommodate Pets on Moving Day

Some companies allow well-behaved dogs to travel in the cab with the driver. Others will happily work around a cat carrier in the front seat. The best ones ask about your pets before you mention them.

3. Knowledge of NZ Pet Transport Regulations

If you\u2019re moving between the North and South Island, your pet may need to cross on the Interislander ferry. A knowledgeable mover understands the pet policies for ferry travel and can advise on the best approach \u2014 whether that\u2019s keeping your pet with you in the passenger area or using the vehicle deck\u2019s pet facilities.

4. Proper Insurance Coverage

Ask specifically whether their insurance covers incidents involving pets during the move. Most don\u2019t, and you\u2019ll need separate pet insurance or a specific rider for moving day.

Most NZ pet owners end up using both \u2014 a pet-friendly furniture mover for belongings and a dedicated pet transport service or self-drive for the animal itself.

New Zealand Pet Transport Services

Here are reputable pet transport options operating across New Zealand:

Airfreight Pet Transport

Air New Zealand operates domestic pet cargo services between major centres. This is often the fastest and least stressful option for long-distance moves, particularly Auckland to Christchurch or Wellington to Dunedin. Pets travel in approved IATA crates in the climate-controlled cargo hold.

  • Best for: Long-distance intercity moves (Auckland\u2013Christchurch, Wellington\u2013Queenstown)
  • Cost: $300\u2013$1,200 depending on crate size and route
  • Booking: Through Air NZ Cargo or pet transport brokers

Professional Pet Transport Companies

Several NZ companies specialise in door-to-door pet transport with climate-controlled vehicles, regular stops, and experienced handlers:

  • Pet Express NZ \u2014 Nationwide coverage, handles both cats and dogs, offers inter-island moves
  • Jetpets \u2014 International and domestic, premium service with vet checks
  • Pet Van Lines \u2014 Ground transport across both islands, regular comfort stops
  • Zoomies Pet Transport \u2014 Smaller operator with personalised service, good for anxious animals

Self-Drive Options

Many pet owners prefer to drive their pets themselves. This gives you full control over temperature, stops, and comfort. For inter-island moves, the Interislander ferry allows pets:

  • Dogs can travel on the vehicle deck in your car (free with vehicle ticket)
  • Cats and small pets need approved carriers in the pet area
  • Pet cabins are available on some sailings (book early \u2014 they sell out)
Important: If you\u2019re moving to the South Island, check local council regulations. Some areas have specific cat containment rules or dog breed restrictions that differ from North Island councils.

Preparing Your Pet for Moving Day

2\u20134 Weeks Before the Move

  • Update microchip details with your new address and phone number
  • Visit the vet for a health check, especially for older pets or those with conditions
  • Get vaccination records \u2014 you may need these for pet transport services or new vet registration
  • Introduce the carrier \u2014 leave it open in the house with treats and blankets inside so your pet gets comfortable
  • Research a new vet in your destination area and book a first appointment

1 Week Before

  • Pack a pet essentials bag: food, medication, favourite toy, leash, water bowl, vet records, and a familiar-smelling blanket
  • Maintain routine as much as possible \u2014 same feeding times, same walk schedule
  • Prepare identification: ensure collar tags have your mobile number (not landline)
  • Arrange a pet sitter for moving day itself \u2014 this is often the best option

Moving Day

  • If your pet stays home: confine them to one quiet room with food, water, and their carrier
  • If travelling with your pet: never leave them in a hot car \u2014 NZ summer temperatures can be lethal
  • Use pheromone sprays (Feliway for cats, Adaptil for dogs) in the carrier and car
  • Feed a light meal 3\u20134 hours before travel to reduce motion sickness
  • Keep cats in carriers at all times during the move \u2014 a frightened cat in a new house will bolt through an open door

Inter-Island Moves: Special Considerations

Moving between the North and South Island adds complexity for pet owners:

Ferry Travel with Pets

  • Interislander: Dogs can stay in your vehicle on the car deck (you can visit during the crossing). Cats and small pets go in the designated pet area. Pet cabins available on some sailings.
  • Bluebridge: Similar pet arrangements. Both ferries require animals to be in approved carriers when not in vehicles.
  • Book early for pet cabins \u2014 they\u2019re limited and popular during peak moving seasons (December\u2013February).

Quarantine and Council Rules

There\u2019s no quarantine requirement for moving pets between North and South Islands. However, some South Island councils have stricter dog registration rules and cat containment bylaws. Check with your destination council before you move.

Pro tip: If you\u2019re moving to Canterbury or Otago, register your dog with the local council within 30 days. Penalties for late registration can be steep \u2014 up to $300 on top of the standard fee.

Settling Your Pet into a New Home

The move is only half the battle. Helping your pet adjust to a new environment takes patience and planning:

First 24 Hours

  • Confine to one room with familiar furniture, smells, and their bed
  • Keep doors closed \u2014 especially external doors \u2014 until your pet is calm and oriented
  • Maintain feeding times from your old schedule to provide continuity
  • Cats: Keep indoors for at least 2\u20133 weeks before letting them explore outside
  • Dogs: Walk the neighbourhood on-leash first so they learn the new territory boundaries

First Week

  • Gradually open up more rooms as your pet seems comfortable
  • Set up their food and water station in a permanent location
  • Introduce neighbourhood sounds gradually \u2014 construction, traffic, other animals
  • Cats may hide for several days \u2014 this is normal. Provide hiding spots and don\u2019t force interaction
  • Dogs may have accidents indoors even if previously housetrained \u2014 be patient and reinforce outdoor toileting
Red flag: If your cat hasn\u2019t eaten or used the litter tray for 48+ hours after a move, contact your vet. Stress-induced hepatic lipidosis can develop quickly in cats and is a medical emergency.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can moving companies transport my pet along with my furniture?

Most furniture removal companies do not transport pets in their trucks. Pets should travel with you, via a dedicated pet transport service, or by air. Some pet-friendly movers may allow small pets in the cab with the driver, but this is the exception rather than the rule.

How much does it cost to transport a pet from Auckland to Christchurch?

Pet transport by air between Auckland and Christchurch typically costs $600\u2013$1,200 depending on the size of your pet and the crate required. Ground transport is usually cheaper ($400\u2013$800) but takes significantly longer.

Do I need any special permits to move my pet between NZ islands?

No special permits are required for domestic pet travel between the North and South Islands. However, you will need an approved travel crate for air or ferry travel, and you must comply with the dog registration requirements of your new local council.

How long should I keep my cat indoors after moving?

At minimum, keep your cat indoors for 2\u20133 weeks after moving. This allows them to fully associate the new house with safety and food. When you do let them out, do so gradually \u2014 initially only under supervision and before feeding time so they have incentive to return.

What if my pet gets lost during a move?

This is why updated microchip details are essential. If your pet escapes during a move, contact the local council animal control, nearby vet clinics, and the SPCA immediately. Post on local community Facebook groups and Neighbourly. Having a recent photo and updated microchip registration dramatically increases reunion rates.
