Overnight Pet Sitting in NZ: What It Involves and How to Choose the Right Sitter

By ZiggySitters NZ  •  March 6, 2026  •  11 min read

Overnight pet sitting — where a professional sitter stays in your home while you’re away — is one of the fastest-growing pet care options in New Zealand. For many pets, it’s the least stressful alternative to kennels or asking a friend to drop in twice a day. This guide explains exactly what it involves, when it makes sense, how much it costs, and how to set your sitter up for success.

What Is Overnight Pet Sitting?

Overnight pet sitting (sometimes called house-sitting with pets, or in-home overnight care) means a trained pet sitter comes to your home, stays through the night, and cares for your pets around the clock. It differs from:

  • Drop-in visits: The sitter visits once or twice a day for 30–60 minutes but does not stay overnight.
  • Dog boarding: Your dog stays at the sitter’s home, not yours.
  • Kennels: Your pet is housed in a commercial facility with many other animals.

With overnight sitting, your pet sleeps in their own bed, eats from their own bowls, and follows their usual routine — just with a trusted sitter rather than you.

What Does an Overnight Pet Sitter Typically Do?

The scope of overnight pet sitting varies by sitter and booking platform, so always confirm exactly what is included before booking. A quality overnight pet sitting service in New Zealand should include:

Evening Arrival

  • Arrive before your pet’s usual evening feeding time
  • Follow your care sheet for feeding amounts and schedules
  • Evening walk or outdoor toilet time for dogs
  • Play or enrichment time before settling for the night
  • Any medications administered as directed

Overnight

  • Sleep in your home (in the guest room, on the sofa, or wherever agreed)
  • Be present for any nighttime needs (senior dogs, anxious pets, puppies)
  • Monitor for signs of illness or distress
  • Send you a settling-in update and photo if requested

Morning Routine

  • Morning feed as per your schedule
  • Morning walk or outdoor access for dogs
  • Litter box serviced for cats
  • A brief update or handover message before departure
  • Home secured when they leave

Who Benefits Most from Overnight Pet Sitting?

Overnight pet sitting is not the only option, but it is genuinely the best option for certain pets and situations.

Dogs Who Should Not Be Left Overnight Alone

  • Dogs with separation anxiety: Being alone overnight amplifies anxiety symptoms. A sitter’s presence can be the difference between a calm dog and a distressed one.
  • Senior dogs: Older dogs often need toilet access more frequently during the night. They also benefit from immediate attention if a health issue arises.
  • Puppies: A puppy left alone overnight can cause significant property damage and will be in genuine distress. Overnight sitting is almost always the right call for dogs under 12 months.
  • Dogs recovering from surgery or illness: Post-operative monitoring is critical. An overnight sitter can catch complications early.
  • Dogs on medications with complex timing: Overnight care ensures medication schedules are followed precisely.

Cats Who Benefit

Cats are often assumed to be self-sufficient for a night, but this is not always true. Consider overnight sitting for:

  • Cats with chronic health conditions (diabetes, kidney disease, hyperthyroidism)
  • Elderly cats with cognitive decline
  • Cats prone to urinary blockages or other acute conditions
  • Multi-cat households where conflict or resource guarding can escalate when owners are absent

Situations Where Overnight Sitting Makes Practical Sense

  • You are away for multiple nights and want daily photo updates
  • You have multiple pets of different species (dog + cat + rabbit, for example)
  • You feel uncomfortable having a complete stranger access your home multiple times while you are away
  • Your neighbourhood or property has specific security considerations
  • You want your home looked after as well as your pets (plants watered, mail collected, lights varied)

Overnight Sitting vs Drop-In Visits vs Kennels

Drop-In Visits

Sitter visits 1–3x daily. Pet is alone the rest of the time.

Best for: Cats and low-anxiety dogs on short trips (1–2 nights).

Typical cost: $25–$50 per visit.

Overnight Sitting ★

Sitter stays in your home. Pet is never alone for extended periods.

Best for: Dogs, anxious pets, seniors, puppies, multi-pet households.

Typical cost: $70–$120 per night.

Kennels / Boarding

Pet stays at external facility with other animals and staff.

Best for: Social dogs who love other dogs and tolerate new environments.

Typical cost: $40–$90 per night.

Overnight pet sitting rates in New Zealand vary by region, sitter experience, number of pets, and the length of your booking. As a general guide:

Additional pets typically add $10–$30 per night to the base rate. Some sitters charge a one-off meet-and-greet or booking fee. Always confirm the full rate inclusive of all pets before booking.

💡 Book Early: Over school holidays (especially the Christmas/New Year break, Easter, and July school holidays), experienced NZ overnight sitters are booked weeks in advance. If you need overnight care for summer, begin searching in October or November.

How to Find a Trusted Overnight Pet Sitter in NZ

Not all pet sitters are equal, and overnight care requires a higher level of trust than a quick drop-in visit. Someone is sleeping in your home. Here is how to find the right person.

1. Use a Platform That Vets Its Sitters

The safest approach is to use a platform like ZiggySitters that screens sitter profiles, collects reviews from verified pet owners, and has a system for resolving disputes. Avoid booking strangers from Facebook Marketplace without reviews or references.

2. Read Reviews Carefully

Look for reviews that mention overnight stays specifically. A sitter with 50 five-star reviews for drop-in visits may not have overnight experience. Filter reviews by service type where the platform allows it.

3. Do a Meet-and-Greet First

A meet-and-greet is a brief, usually free, in-person visit where your potential sitter meets your pets in your home before the booking. It serves two purposes: you assess the sitter, and your pet meets them in a neutral, low-stakes environment.

During the meet-and-greet, note:

  • Does the sitter interact naturally with your pet, or seem nervous or hesitant?
  • Do they ask good questions about your pet’s routine, history, and preferences?
  • Are they comfortable in your home, and do you feel comfortable with them in it?
  • Does your pet respond positively to them?

4. Ask the Right Questions

Before confirming a booking, ask your prospective overnight sitter:

  • “Have you done overnight stays before? How many and what types of pets?”
  • “What would you do if my dog became ill or showed signs of distress at 2am?”
  • “Are you comfortable administering medications?”
  • “Will you be the only person in my home, or might others join you?”
  • “Do you have a first aid certificate or any pet first aid training?”
  • “How will you communicate updates with me during the stay?”

5. Check Their References

For overnight bookings, it is entirely reasonable to ask for contact details of previous clients who can speak to their experience. A confident, experienced sitter will be happy to provide references.

How to Prepare Your Home for an Overnight Sitter

You can make the overnight experience much smoother for your sitter — and therefore better for your pet — with good preparation.

Leave a Comprehensive Care Sheet

A written care sheet is the single most important thing you can leave your sitter. Include:

  • Feeding schedule, amounts, and any food sensitivities
  • Medication instructions with timing and dosage clearly stated
  • Usual exercise routine and favourite walks or play activities
  • Sleeping arrangements (where does your pet sleep? Is furniture allowed?)
  • Known quirks, fears, or triggers (vacuum cleaners, other dogs, strangers)
  • Emergency contact: your mobile number, a local backup contact, and your vet clinic details
  • After-hours emergency vet: the nearest 24-hour veterinary clinic to your address

Prepare the Physical Space

  • Show your sitter where pet food, leads, bowls, medications, and cleaning supplies are stored
  • Leave the guest room or sleeping area set up and welcoming
  • Ensure the property is pet-safe: put away anything toxic, secure pool gates, check fence integrity
  • Leave your wi-fi password written somewhere visible
  • Show them how to operate your security alarm if you have one
  • Leave a house key in the way you agreed, plus a backup if possible

Emergency Preparedness

Your sitter should know exactly what to do in an emergency. Leave written authority for them to authorise emergency veterinary treatment on your behalf, along with a rough budget limit you are comfortable approving remotely if you cannot be reached immediately. Many NZ vet clinics require owner authorisation before expensive procedures.

⚠️ Important: Make sure your pet’s microchip details and NZCAR registration are current before any overnight stay. If your pet were to escape from the property, up-to-date registration is the fastest route to a reunion. Check your registration at nzcar.net.nz.

Overnight Sitter Checklist for Pet Owners

  • Book at least 2–3 weeks in advance (longer during school holidays)
  • Complete the meet-and-greet before the first overnight stay
  • Prepare a written care sheet with all essential information
  • Show sitter the full property, including gates, fences, and alarms
  • Confirm sleeping arrangements and any house rules
  • Leave emergency vet details and written treatment authority
  • Agree on a communication plan: how often, what format, what to report immediately
  • Ensure microchip and council registration are current
  • Leave a contact for a local backup person (in case sitter has an emergency)
  • Confirm full cost before the stay begins

What Happens If Something Goes Wrong?

Booking through a reputable platform like ZiggySitters gives you a structured process if issues arise. Document any concerns with photos and contact the platform’s support team promptly. Most platforms have policies covering property damage, pet injury, and sitter no-shows.

For genuine pet health emergencies, your sitter should contact you immediately and proceed to the emergency vet if you cannot be reached within a reasonable timeframe. This is why the written emergency authorisation is so important.

Overnight Pet Sitting FAQs

What hours does overnight sitting typically cover?

Most NZ overnight sitters arrive between 5–7pm and depart between 7–9am the following morning. This gives approximately 12–15 hours of in-home coverage. Confirm exact arrival and departure times with your sitter before booking.

Can my sitter bring their own pet?

Some sitters may request to bring a companion dog or cat. Always discuss this in advance and decide whether it is appropriate for your pet. A well-matched companion can be a positive experience; a poorly matched one can cause significant stress. If in doubt, request a sitter without an accompanying animal.

Do I need to pay more for multiple pets?

Most NZ overnight sitters charge an additional fee for each pet beyond the first. This is standard practice. Confirm the full multi-pet rate at the time of booking rather than assuming it is included.

Is my home insured during an overnight sit?

Your standard home and contents insurance should remain active during an overnight sit, provided you have informed your insurer that a third party will be staying in your home. Check your policy or contact your insurer to confirm coverage specifics before your trip.

Final Thoughts

Overnight pet sitting is one of the most considered and caring choices you can make for a pet who genuinely needs company and routine while you are away. Done well — with the right sitter, a thorough care sheet, and a clear handover — your pet will experience minimal disruption and you will travel with genuine peace of mind.

New Zealand has a growing community of professional, experienced overnight pet sitters. Take the time to find one who is the right fit for your pet, do the meet-and-greet, and invest in preparation. The result will be a calm, well-cared-for animal waiting for your return.
