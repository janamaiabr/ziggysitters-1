Pet-Friendly Shopping in NZ: Malls, Markets & Garden Centres That Welcome Pets

Published 24 July 2026 — ZiggySitters NZ

Running errands with your dog should not mean leaving them at home alone for hours. Across New Zealand, a growing number of shopping centres, farmers markets, garden centres, and retail stores are opening their doors to well-behaved pets. This guide covers where you can shop with your pet, how to be a welcome guest, and what to pack for a smooth outing.

The Rules: What NZ Law Says About Pets in Shops

In New Zealand, there is no general law granting pets access to retail spaces. Individual businesses decide their own pet policy. The only legal requirement is that working assistance dogs (guide dogs, hearing dogs, and disability assistance dogs) must be allowed entry under the Human Rights Act 1993. Emotional support animals and therapy pets do not have the same legal access rights, though many businesses welcome them voluntarily.

Food preparation and food sales areas are subject to food safety regulations, which generally exclude animals. However, the definition of a "food preparation area" varies — a cafe with table service is different from a supermarket. When in doubt, ask before entering.

Pet-Friendly Retailers in New Zealand

Hardware and Garden Centres

Hardware stores are among the most consistently pet-friendly retailers in NZ. They have open layouts, non-food inventory, and a culture of dogs accompanying tradies on the job.

Mitre 10 — Most Mitre 10 stores across NZ welcome leashed dogs. The garden centre sections are particularly popular for a wander with your pet. Individual stores set their own policies, so check with your local branch.
Bunnings Warehouse — Bunnings has a national policy allowing well-behaved, leashed dogs in-store (excluding the trade drive-through area). This makes Bunnings one of the most reliable pet-friendly shopping destinations in New Zealand. Staff often keep dog treats at the service desk.
PlaceMakers — Generally dog-friendly, though individual store policies may vary. Smaller branches in pet-heavy communities (like those near beaches or walking tracks) tend to be more welcoming.

Pet Supply Stores

Unsurprisingly, stores that sell pet products welcome pets inside. This is also a great socialisation opportunity for puppies and kittens.

Animate — All Animate stores welcome pets. They offer free weigh-ins, and many locations have community events and adoption days.
Petstock — Pets are welcome in all Petstock stores. Staff are accustomed to animals and can help with product fitting (harnesses, life jackets, coats) right in-store.
K9 Natural & Feline Natural flagship — The Christchurch flagship store is fully pet-friendly and offers tastings for dogs.

Bookshops and Boutique Retail

Independent bookshops, particularly in smaller towns, often have resident cats and welcome visiting dogs. Some Wellington and Auckland bookshops have become informal community gathering spots for dog owners.

Unity Books (Wellington and Auckland) — Known for their pet-friendly approach and water bowls at the door.
Scorpio Books (Christchurch) — Small, independent, and dog-welcoming.
Volume Bookshop (Nelson) — A community favourite that welcomes well-behaved dogs.

Farmers Markets and Sunday Markets

Outdoor markets are generally more pet-friendly than indoor retail, though food stall areas may have restrictions.

  • La Cigale French Market (Auckland) — Dogs welcome on leash in the outdoor area
  • Wellington Underground Market — Dogs welcome in the outdoor section
  • Christchurch Farmers Market (Riccarton House) — Leashed dogs welcome on the grounds
  • Nelson Market — Dogs on leash are welcome throughout
  • Hawke Bay Farmers Market (Hastings) — Pet-friendly outdoor market
  • Otago Farmers Market (Dunedin) — Leashed dogs welcome

Shopping Malls

Most enclosed shopping malls in NZ do not allow pets (excluding assistance dogs). However, there are exceptions and some open-air retail precincts that do:

  • Takanini, South Auckland — Some outdoor-access stores at The Depot accept leashed pets
  • Britomart (Auckland CBD) — The outdoor courtyard areas between shops are accessible with leashed dogs
  • Lantern Shopping Centre (Various) — Policies vary by location
Always check before visiting a mall. Policies change, and security guards will ask you to leave if pets are not permitted. When in doubt, call ahead or check the mall website.

Pet Shopping Etiquette: Being a Welcome Guest

The reason some stores reverse pet-friendly policies is bad behaviour from a few pet owners. Follow these rules to keep doors open for everyone:

Before You Go

  • Confirm the policy. Call or check the store website. Policies can vary between branches of the same chain.
  • Exercise your dog first. A tired dog is a calm dog. A 20-minute walk before shopping makes the experience much better for both of you.
  • Bring your own supplies. Carry waste bags, a portable water bowl, and treats. Do not expect the store to supply these.
  • Check the weather. If it is hot, leave your dog at home. Even with windows cracked, cars become dangerously hot within minutes in NZ summer.

In the Store

  • Keep your dog on a short leash — not a retractable lead. You need full control in tight aisles.
  • Stay close to your dog at all times. Do not let them wander around corners or out of sight.
  • Do not let your dog sniff or lick merchandise. This is a health and hygiene issue, and it is the fastest way to lose pet access.
  • Respect other shoppers. Not everyone loves dogs. Keep your pet close and away from people who seem uncomfortable.
  • Clean up immediately if your dog has an accident. Carry pet-safe cleaning wipes and inform staff so they can sanitise the area.
  • Do not bring reactive dogs. If your dog barks at strangers, lunges, or becomes anxious in crowded spaces, shopping trips are not appropriate socialisation. Work on behaviour first with a trainer.

What to Pack for a Shopping Trip with Your Pet

Pet Shopping Kit:
  • Waste bags (at least 4 — you may need more than you think)
  • Portable water bowl and water bottle
  • Treats for rewarding calm behaviour
  • Pet-safe cleaning wipes for accidents
  • A small towel for wiping paws (especially in wet NZ weather)
  • Current vaccination records on your phone (photo or vet app)
  • A well-fitted harness (safer than a collar in crowded spaces)
  • A mat or blanket if your dog will be waiting outside (never in a car)

Pet-Friendly Shopping by Region

Auckland

Auckland has the most pet-friendly shopping options in NZ, concentrated in the inner-city suburbs and coastal areas. Britomart and Wynyard Quarter have several pet-friendly outdoor spaces. Most Bunnings and Mitre 10 stores in the Auckland region welcome dogs. The weekly Clevedon Village Farmers Market allows leashed dogs.

Wellington

Wellington is NZ most dog-friendly city for shopping. The waterfront, Cuba Street outdoor areas, and numerous independent retailers welcome dogs. Unity Books, Mojo Coffee locations, and the waterfront markets are all reliable choices. The Wellington Underground Market allows leashed dogs in outdoor sections.

Christchurch

Post-earthquake Christchurch has embraced outdoor, community-oriented retail. The Riverside Market outdoor areas, Riccarton House grounds (Saturday farmers market), and The Tannery all welcome leashed dogs. Many New Regent Street cafes have outdoor seating that accommodates pets.

Smaller Towns and Rural Areas

Smaller NZ towns tend to be more pet-friendly overall. In places like Raglan, Wanaka, and Akaroa, many shops have water bowls at the door and dog treats on the counter. Rural supply stores (Farmlands, PGG Wrightson) almost always welcome dogs, as their customer base is predominantly farmers and rural dog owners.

FAQ

Can I take my cat shopping?

Technically yes, if the store allows pets, but most cats find shopping environments extremely stressful. Unlike dogs, cats rarely enjoy novel environments with strangers and noise. Unless your cat is unusually calm and comfortable in a carrier, it is kinder to leave them at home.

Are puppies allowed in pet-friendly stores?

Pet-friendly stores generally welcome puppies, but only after they have completed their full vaccination course (typically 12-16 weeks). Before that, puppies are vulnerable to diseases like parvovirus and should avoid public floors and contact with unknown dogs.

What about cats in pet supply stores?

Most pet supply stores (Animate, Petstock) welcome cats in carriers. Some even have a safe space where you can let your cat out briefly for product fitting. Always call ahead to confirm.

Can businesses ask me to leave because of my dog?

Yes. Private businesses have the right to refuse entry to pets at any time, for any reason — unless your dog is a registered assistance dog. If asked to leave, comply politely. Arguing only hurts the chances of pet-friendly policies continuing.

Can I take my dog into a shopping centre food court?

Generally no. Food courts are classified as food preparation and consumption areas, and food safety regulations exclude animals (except assistance dogs). Outdoor dining areas at individual restaurants may allow pets at the manager discretion.
