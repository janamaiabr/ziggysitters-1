NZ Public Holidays Pet Care: Planning Guide 2026

By ZiggySitters NZ · Updated March 2026 · 7 min read

New Zealand’s public holidays and long weekends are some of the most popular travel times of the year — and the most stressful for pet owners who need to sort last-minute care. Whether you’re heading to a family Easter gathering, a Waitangi Day road trip, or a Christmas holiday in the Coromandel, this guide will help you plan your pet’s care well in advance and avoid the holiday booking rush.

Why Public Holiday Pet Care Is Different

During regular weekdays and weekends, finding a quality pet sitter is usually straightforward. Public holidays change the equation completely:

  • Demand spikes sharply. Tens of thousands of New Zealanders travel during Easter, Christmas, and Anzac Day long weekends simultaneously. Pet sitter availability is limited.
  • Sitters book out weeks or months ahead. Experienced, well-reviewed sitters — the ones you actually want — are often fully booked 4–8 weeks before major holidays.
  • Vet clinics have reduced hours. Most vet practices in NZ are closed or on skeleton staff during public holidays. If your pet has a medical issue, your options narrow fast.
  • Your pet’s routine is already disrupted. Visitors, noise, fireworks (Guy Fawkes), and changed household rhythms can stress even calm, confident pets.
Key fact: In a ZiggySitters survey, over 60% of NZ pet owners who struggled to find holiday pet care said they started looking less than two weeks before the holiday. Don’t be in that group — start early.

Holiday-by-Holiday Pet Care Considerations

🏳️ Waitangi Day (February)

A long weekend in peak NZ summer. Many families head to beaches or whare tupuna visits up north. February is also NZ’s hottest month in most regions — heat safety is a priority for any pet left in your care. Make sure your sitter knows to avoid midday walks and to keep fresh water topped up constantly. Book at least 3–4 weeks ahead.

🎁 Easter (April)

Easter is New Zealand’s busiest domestic travel period. Four days off, school holidays often aligned, and perfect autumn weather make it the peak of peak. Book your pet sitter 6–8 weeks out if you want any choice. There is another critical reason to plan carefully: Easter means chocolate. Chocolate is toxic to dogs and cats — ensure your sitter knows to keep Easter eggs completely out of reach and to contact you or an emergency vet if any is consumed.

  • Chocolate toxicity symptoms in dogs: vomiting, restlessness, rapid breathing, muscle tremors
  • Xylitol (in some sugar-free Easter sweets) is even more dangerous — can cause liver failure
  • Easter lilies are highly toxic to cats — common in Easter arrangements

🥊 Anzac Day (April)

Anzac Day dawn services often involve early morning activity and community gatherings. Many Kiwis combine the Anzac weekend with travel. Demand for pet sitters is high but slightly lower than Easter. Book 3–4 weeks ahead. Autumn weather means milder conditions for walks — a good time for active pets who struggled with the summer heat.

🌟 Matariki (June/July)

New Zealand’s newest public holiday celebrates the Māori New Year. Mid-winter timing means cold nights for outdoor pets — ensure your sitter has clear instructions about bringing animals indoors or providing adequate outdoor shelter. Matariki is still building in popularity as a travel period, so sitter availability is generally better than Easter or Christmas.

🎉 Christmas and New Year (December – January)

The most demanding pet care period of the year, full stop. The Christmas to New Year stretch — often extended to 2–3 weeks as families return to home cities or head overseas — creates the highest demand for pet sitters in NZ. Top-rated sitters on ZiggySitters are regularly fully booked from October onwards for Christmas. If you have a holiday planned, book before you book your flights.

Additional considerations for the Christmas period:

  • Festive foods: Grapes, raisins, macadamia nuts, onion (all in typical Christmas meals) are toxic to dogs. Brief your sitter.
  • Fireworks (New Year’s Eve): NZ allows personal fireworks sales around Guy Fawkes (November) but New Year’s Eve also brings professional displays. Anxious pets need a plan — see our fireworks and pet anxiety guide.
  • Heat: December–January is peak summer. Heat safety protocols are essential.

How to Book a Holiday Pet Sitter: Step-by-Step

  1. Search early. As soon as your travel is likely (even before firm bookings), start browsing sitter profiles.
  2. Read reviews carefully. Look for sitters who mention holiday experience or who have been booked over previous long weekends.
  3. Request a meet-and-greet. Always meet your potential sitter in person before the holiday, especially for longer stays. Your pet needs to be comfortable with them.
  4. Confirm vet access. Provide your vet’s contact details and pre-authorise emergency treatment in writing. Holiday vet emergencies happen — your sitter needs to be able to act fast.
  5. Prepare a care sheet. Document feeding times, medical requirements, behavioural quirks, favourite toys, and any “do not give” foods. See our pet care sheet guide.
  6. Confirm again 1 week before. A quick check-in message ensures nothing has changed and gives you peace of mind.
Tip: If you book the same sitter each holiday, keep their contact details saved and reach out directly before they open availability publicly. Regulars often get first pick — and pets who already know their sitter settle in much faster.

What to Prepare Before You Leave

Once your sitter is booked, a little preparation makes the stay far smoother for everyone:

For Your Pet

  • Ensure vaccinations and parasite treatments are up to date
  • Leave enough food for the stay, plus a 2-day buffer in case of delays
  • Include familiar bedding or a worn item of clothing with your scent to comfort anxious pets
  • Check that ID tags and microchip details are current (confirm with the NZCAR register)
  • Leave any medications clearly labelled with dosage instructions

For Your Sitter

  • Emergency vet contact (nearest 24-hour clinic if applicable)
  • Your mobile number and that of a local backup contact
  • Home access: keys, alarm codes, gate combos — tested before you leave
  • Wi-Fi password if they are doing an in-home stay
  • Any known phobias or triggers (thunderstorms, strangers, loud noises)

Special Considerations: Regional Anniversary Days

New Zealand has eleven regional public holidays that most of the country often overlooks. Each region observes its own anniversary day — Auckland Anniversary (late January), Wellington Anniversary (late January), Canterbury Anniversary (November), Otago Anniversary (March), and so on. These create local mini-peaks in pet care demand that are easy to miss if you are not from the region.

If you are travelling during a regional anniversary day, check local sitter availability 3–4 weeks ahead. Because these are region-specific, demand can be intense in the local market even though it is invisible nationally.

What If Your Pet Has Anxiety?

Holidays already disrupt pets’ routines. If your dog or cat has known anxiety, extra planning is warranted:

  • Choose an in-home sitter who stays at your property rather than a boarder or drop-in visitor — familiar surroundings help enormously.
  • Brief your sitter on your pet’s specific triggers and what to do when anxiety spikes.
  • Talk to your vet before the holiday about short-term anxiety management options (supplements, pheromone diffusers, or short-course medication).
  • Arrange a practice stay before the actual holiday — one or two nights with the sitter while you are still in Auckland, Wellington, or wherever you live, so your pet learns the sitter is safe.
Public Holiday Pet Care: Quick Checklist
  • ✅ Book your sitter early — 6–8 weeks for Easter and Christmas
  • ✅ Meet-and-greet before the stay
  • ✅ Written care sheet with feeding, medical, and behaviour notes
  • ✅ Emergency vet details provided and treatment pre-authorised
  • ✅ Up-to-date ID tag and microchip registration
  • ✅ Food supply plus 2-day buffer
  • ✅ Holiday-specific hazards noted (chocolate at Easter, heat in summer, fireworks on NYE)
  • ✅ Confirm booking 1 week before departure

Final Thoughts

New Zealand’s public holidays are a chance to relax, reconnect with family, and explore this beautiful country. They should not be a source of anxiety about your pets. The difference between a stressful last-minute scramble and a smooth, worry-free holiday comes down to one thing: booking your pet care early.

Use the timeline in this guide, prepare a thorough care sheet, and choose a sitter your pet already knows. Do that, and you can head off on your long weekend knowing your furry family member is in the best possible hands.
