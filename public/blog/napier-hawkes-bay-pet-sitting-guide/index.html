Pet Sitting in Napier & Hawke's Bay 2026: Your Complete Local Guide

By ZiggySitters • March 3, 2026 • 11 min read

Napier and Hawke's Bay offer one of New Zealand's most pet-friendly lifestyles. With long sunny beaches, spacious parks, a relaxed regional pace, and a tight-knit community feel, it's a wonderful place to be a pet owner. But when work trips, holidays, or unexpected events mean you need someone to care for your animals, knowing who to trust is everything.

This guide covers everything Hawke's Bay pet owners need to know: local dog parks and beaches, trusted vet clinics, and how to find and vet the right pet sitter for your family.

Why Hawke's Bay Is a Great Place for Pets

Hawke's Bay's warm, dry climate — the sunniest region in the North Island — means your pets can enjoy outdoor life for most of the year. The region stretches from Napier and Hastings on the coast to rural areas like Wairoa and Central Hawke's Bay, offering very different lifestyles for both urban and rural pet owners.

Napier itself is a compact, walkable city with multiple off-leash parks and easy access to the beach. Hastings offers a more suburban feel with larger properties and excellent local vet services. The region's relatively low population density compared to Auckland or Wellington means less traffic risk for pets and a generally calmer environment.

😊 Hawke's Bay Pet Ownership Snapshot Hawke's Bay consistently ranks among the higher pet-owning regions in New Zealand per capita. Dogs are particularly popular given the outdoor lifestyle, and local councils have invested significantly in off-leash areas and dog exercise parks in recent years.

Dog-Friendly Spots in Napier & Hastings

Before you travel, knowing where your pet sitter can take your dog for proper exercise is an important part of their care plan. Here are the main dog-friendly destinations in Hawke's Bay:

Off-Leash Dog Parks in Napier

  • Nelson Park Off-Leash Area — One of Napier's most popular, well-fenced with room to run. Located centrally in the Napier urban area.
  • Clive Square Dog Exercise Area — Smaller but centrally located. Good for a quick morning run before work.
  • Westshore Beach — Dogs are permitted off-leash on sections of Westshore beach before 10am and after 5pm during summer. Always verify current rules with Napier City Council before visiting.
  • Napier Port Beach Area — The foreshore near Marine Parade offers excellent walking routes, with leash rules varying by section.

Dog-Friendly Areas in Hastings

  • Frimley Park Dog Exercise Area — Large, fenced off-leash area within Frimley Park. Popular with local dog owners.
  • Flaxmere Park — Spacious green areas suitable for exercise. Check current dog rules with Hastings District Council.
  • Haumoana and Te Awanga Beaches — Beach access south of Hastings. Dogs are generally permitted but always check current seasonal restrictions.
⚠ Always Check Current Rules Dog access rules change seasonally and can be updated by council at any time. Before sending your sitter somewhere new, confirm current rules at the Napier City Council or Hastings District Council websites. Fines for dogs in restricted areas apply to whoever has control of the dog at the time.

Vet Clinics in Hawke's Bay

Every pet owner should have their regular vet and an after-hours emergency vet contact ready before they leave on a trip. Share both with your pet sitter as a non-negotiable part of your care handover.

Napier Vets

  • Napier Veterinary Centre — Established local practice in Napier, catering to companion animals.
  • Hawke's Bay Animal Hospital — Larger facility with a broader range of specialist services.
  • VetEnt Napier — Part of the national VetEnt group, offering consistent standards and extended hours at some locations.

Hastings Vets

  • Hastings Veterinary Centre — Well-regarded local practice serving Hastings and surrounding suburbs.
  • Stortford Lodge Veterinary Clinic — Serves the northern Hastings and Havelock North area.
  • Havelock North Veterinary Clinic — Popular with the Havelock North community.
Tip: Emergency Vet Contact Hawke's Bay does not have a dedicated 24-hour emergency-only vet clinic. Most practices have an after-hours on-call service. Make sure you know your regular vet's after-hours number AND the nearest 24-hour emergency clinic (Palmerston North or Tauranga for serious cases). Share this information clearly with your pet sitter.

Types of Pet Sitting Services Available in Napier & Hawke's Bay

Not all pet sitting services are the same. Understanding your options helps you choose what suits your pet's needs and your peace of mind.

In-Home Pet Sitting

The sitter comes to your home, maintaining your pet's normal routine in their familiar environment. This is usually the least stressful option for most pets, especially cats, anxious dogs, senior animals, or pets with medical needs. Your pet isn't exposed to other animals, and your home is monitored while you're away.

Drop-In Visits

The sitter visits your home 1–3 times daily for feeding, play, and a welfare check. Suitable for cats and some dogs, particularly if a trusted family member or neighbour can supplement the visits. Not suitable for puppies, high-energy dogs, or anxious animals who need human company throughout the day.

Sitter's Home Boarding

Your pet stays at the sitter's home. This can work well for sociable dogs who enjoy company. Always visit the sitter's home first to assess the environment, meet any other animals, and ensure the space is secure and appropriate for your pet.

Dog Walking

Regular walks while you're at work or away. Some Hawke's Bay walkers offer group walks (more economical but less individual attention) or solo walks (more expensive but tailored to your dog's pace and needs).

Best For: Anxious Pets

In-home sitting. Maintains routine, familiar smells, no strange animals.

Best For: Cats

Drop-in visits. Cats prefer their own territory over strange environments.

Best For: Social Dogs

Sitter's home boarding. They enjoy company and do well in new environments.

Best For: High-Energy Dogs

In-home sitting with multiple daily walks. Maintains exercise routine.

How to Choose a Pet Sitter in Hawke's Bay

The Hawke's Bay pet sitting market has grown significantly in recent years. Here is a systematic approach to finding someone you can genuinely trust with your animals.

Step 1: Define What Your Pet Actually Needs

Before you search, write down your pet's specific requirements: medication schedules, dietary needs, exercise requirements, anxiety triggers, and health conditions. This list becomes your filter — you need a sitter who can meet every item, not just most of them.

Step 2: Ask for References — and Actually Check Them

Any experienced pet sitter should be able to provide two or three references from local Hawke's Bay clients who have used their services multiple times. Call them. Ask specific questions: Did the sitter follow your instructions? Were there any incidents? Would you use them again without hesitation?

Step 3: Request a Meet and Greet

Always arrange a meet and greet before committing to a booking. This is standard practice. Observe how the sitter interacts with your pet — do they get down to the animal's level? Do they read body language correctly? Does your pet respond positively to them?

Step 4: Discuss Emergency Protocols

Ask directly: “If my pet became seriously unwell at 2am, what would you do?” They should have a clear answer: your nominated vet, your after-hours vet, and a protocol for reaching you. Vague answers here are a red flag.

Step 5: Confirm Communication Style

How often will they send updates? What format — photos, videos, a daily text? Most Hawke's Bay pet owners travelling domestically or internationally appreciate at least a morning and evening check-in. Set expectations clearly before you leave.

Red Flags to Watch For Be cautious of sitters who: cannot provide local references; are unwilling to do a meet and greet; have no basic first aid knowledge; seem overwhelmed by your pet's care requirements; or are dramatically cheaper than the market rate without explanation.

What to Prepare for Your Pet Sitter

A great sitter can only do their job well if you set them up for success. Before any trip, prepare a clear care document that includes:

  • Feeding schedule with exact amounts and any dietary restrictions
  • Medication details: what, how much, when, and how to administer
  • Walk routes your dog knows and enjoys
  • Your pet's anxiety triggers and calming strategies
  • Your regular vet's name, clinic, and phone number
  • After-hours emergency vet contact
  • Your contact details and a backup contact (family member or friend)
  • Vet authorisation: written permission for the sitter to approve treatment up to a set dollar amount in an emergency
  • Location of pet supplies: food, leads, toys, medication
  • Nearest 24-hour pharmacy (for sitters managing medical pets)

Seasonal Considerations for Pet Care in Hawke's Bay

Hawke's Bay's climate has some seasonal pet care considerations worth noting:

Summer (December – February)

Hawke's Bay summers can be hot and dry. Ensure your sitter knows your pet should have constant access to shade and fresh water. Mid-day walks on hot days should be avoided — concrete pavements can burn paw pads. Early morning or evening walks are best. Brachycephalic breeds (pugs, French bulldogs) need extra monitoring in heat.

Autumn (March – May)

Harvest season in wine country often means increased traffic and machinery in rural areas. If you live near vineyards or orchards, be aware that grape and grape product toxicity is serious for dogs. Fallen fruit in gardens is a hazard worth mentioning to your sitter.

Winter (June – August)

Hawke's Bay winters are mild by New Zealand standards but can bring frosts and cold snaps. Older pets, short-coated breeds, and very young animals may need extra warmth. Ensure indoor access or adequate bedding is available.

Spring (September – November)

Spring brings new plant growth and increased garden chemical use. Slug bait (containing metaldehyde) is extremely toxic to dogs and cats. If your garden or neighbours' gardens use slug bait, alert your sitter. Spring also sees increased wasp activity, particularly in fruit-growing areas — a risk for curious dogs and cats.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does pet sitting cost in Napier?

Typical rates in Hawke's Bay for in-home pet sitting range from $30–$60 per visit or $60–$120 per day for overnight stays. Drop-in visits are generally $20–$40 each. Rates vary based on the number of pets, special needs, and the sitter's experience.

Do I need to use a platform or can I find a sitter privately?

Both options work. A platform like ZiggySitters provides reviewed sitters and a layer of accountability. Private arrangements can work well if you have strong personal references, but always do thorough due diligence regardless of how you find your sitter.

What if my pet needs medication while I'm away?

Many experienced pet sitters are comfortable administering oral medications and some topical treatments. Injections (such as insulin for diabetic pets) require more specific experience — always confirm this ability clearly before booking and arrange a demonstration before you travel.
